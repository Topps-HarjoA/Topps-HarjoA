Unit 7

Lamination and Vacuum Forming


In Unit 7 we made skateboards using Lamination and Vacuum Forming so the normal scaket board can get its curvy shape.

Lamination and Vacuum Forming

Although I did not make a regular skateboard but instead a long board I faced separate challenges in forming my board because I did not use Vacuum Forming but pressure from two pieces of wood. First lets start at the beginning, I made my board from thin pieces of wood which I quickly glued together with wood glue and then placed my board between pieces of wood and with 4 clamps kept the board together. Then I let the glue dry for more than 24 hours. I got my board after it dried and when taking off the wood pushing the thin pieces of wood together my board cracked. I had to fix the cracks and also fix the parts of my board which was not connected causing me to wait another 24 hours before I could clean up my board. After fixing my board with sanding and cutting down rough ends I started my staining process. I stained my board with Black stainer and while the stain was drying I created a sticker for my board. Once my board was dry I added my sticker which said never settle and then applied a clear protective coat to hold everything together.
