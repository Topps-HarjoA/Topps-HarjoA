Electronics

Capacitor - stores eectrical energy in an electric field
Transistor - used to amplify or switch electronic signals and electrical power.
Resistor - a designed resistance to the passage of an electric current.
Led - a light-emitting diode and is a semiconductor light source that emits light when current flows throught it.
Diode - a semiconductor device with two terminal
Potentiometer - is a three terminal resistor with a sliding or rotating contact that forms an adjustable voltage divider.
Breadboard - is a solderless device for temporary prototype with electonics and test circuit designs.
Multimeter - is an electronic measuring instrument that combines several measurement functions in one unit.
Switch - a device for making and breaking the connection in an electric circuit.
The difference between Voltage, Current and Resistance is that voltage is the difference in charge between two points. Current is the rate at which charge is flowing. Resistance is a material's tendency to resist the flow of change of charge also known as current.
The the difference between AC and DC is that the alternating current is when the direction of electricity flow is constantly reversed througout the circuit. While a direct circuit is when electricity flows in one direction between power and ground.
